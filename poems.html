Javelina

We return red, dirty, and decidedly content.
Chris is limping and we carry his gear.
With another mat to carry on my small frame
the setting sun turns me into a black box.

Without fear, or perhaps aware that
we spent our energy pebble wrestling,
the javelina dares to look. To run by us
knowing damn well we won't chase.

Ordeal

Into the slot I reach.
Reach and only sand greets me with his panic.
Manic. Deep breaths, glance down, feet flailing.
My mind at odds with the words from the security of the ground.
The sound of beta and spray. Is that French I hear?

I wipe my hand on my shirt, reach again and improbably the sand has multiplied.
The pump wins and down we ago. Enjoy the ride.

Ole Betsy

The name was tongue in cheek, but by the end of the trip she was no joke.

Fifty miles west of Jasper the tire of my 09 Chevy Aveo blew out. Went kaput. On the side of the highway
in the oppressive southern heat we changed it with one of those spare tires riddled with warnings:
"DO NOT EXCEED FIFTY-FIVE MPH" and "NOT TO BE USED FOR MORE THAN 200 MILES."
We named the tire Ole Betsy and it was her time to shine.

Only the hubris of teenagers and dreams of witnessing the fitness of a grainy Sharma giving a glorious "pssaaat!" kept us going.

Down into the canyon we ventured, the highway leading to an unpaved road and finally into the valley.

For three days we battled with sandstone until finally we had to rely on her one more time
to get us out of the valley past the unpaved road, and back to the highway — finally home.
